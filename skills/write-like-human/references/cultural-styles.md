## Tech/Dev

Mix of technical precision with extremely casual language. Anti-corporate. Self-deprecating.

**Vocabulary:** "ship it," "LGTM," "nuke it," "yak shaving," "bikeshedding," "rubber ducking," "it works on my machine," "tech debt," "spaghetti code," "footgun," "TIL," "FWIW," "YMMV"

**Patterns:**
- Inline code formatting in casual writing: "just run `npm install` and pray"
- Self-deprecating humor: "I've been debugging this for 6 hours. It was a missing semicolon."
- Understatement: "Minor issue: the database is on fire."
- Use of "TL;DR" to summarize
- Direct and concise - don't waste words
- Hot takes on tools and frameworks welcome
- Debugging war stories are cultural currency

**Example:** "Spent 6 hours debugging. The issue? A missing semicolon. I love this profession."

## Crypto/Web3

High-energy, meme-heavy, tribal, urgent. Terse and ironic.

**Core vocab:** gm, gn, wagmi, ngmi, ser, anon, fren, wen, LFG, DYOR, NFA, HODL, FOMO, FUD

**Trading vocab:** diamond hands 💎🙌, paper hands, ape in, rekt, degen, alpha, cope/copium, rug/rug pull, ATH

**Cultural phrases:**
- "probably nothing" (meaning very significant)
- "few" (short for "few understand")
- "this is the way"
- "wen moon" / "wen lambo"

**Style rules:**
- 1–2 word sentences common
- Heavy irony
- Meme-driven references
- Lowercase preferred
- Emoji-heavy: 🔥💀👀🫡📈📉
- .eth handles and NFT PFPs
- GM/GN rituals
- Alpha drops in numbered threads

**Example:** "gm. ETH looking bullish. If you're not accumulating here, ngmi ser."

## Gen Z

Anti-formality as identity. Punctuation carries emotional weight.

**Rules:**
- All lowercase: "i don't think that's a good idea"
- No periods (periods = passive aggression): "ok." = angry; "ok" = fine
- Extended letters for emphasis: "sooooo good" / "nooooo" / "pleaseeee"
- Abbreviations: tbh, ngl, fr, imo, idk, idc, istg, ong, wdym
- Slang: slay, no cap, bussin, fire, mid, sus, bet, based, ratio'd, it's giving, living rent free, main character energy, understood the assignment, ate, devoured

**Tone indicators** (tag the end of ambiguous statements):
- /s = sarcasm
- /j = joking
- /gen = genuine
- /srs = serious
- /pos = positive
- /neg = negative
- /lh = lighthearted

**Emoji meanings that differ from adult interpretation:**
- 😭 = laughing (not crying)
- 💀 or 🪦 = died laughing
- 🤡 = self-mockery ("I'm the clown")
- 🙂 = passive aggressive
- 😊 = unsettling/ironic

**Example:** "ngl this is actually so good?? like i was not expecting that at all tbh"

## Startup/Founder

Build-in-public energy. Vulnerability combined with hustle. Metrics everywhere.

**Vocabulary:** "ship fast," "10x," "disrupt," "scale," "iterate," "pivot," "product-market fit," "first principles," "north star metric," "moat," "ARR/MRR," "zero to one," "default alive/dead"

**Writing patterns:**
- Short declarative sentences
- Numbered lists
- Story format: struggle → insight → success
- Heavy metrics: "$0 to $14K MRR in 6 months"
- Aspirational framing
- Humility-brags: "We almost died in Month 2. Here's what saved us."
- Monthly updates with real numbers
- Rejection stories turned into lessons

**Example:** "Month 6: $8K MRR to $14K MRR. What changed? We stopped building features nobody asked for."

## South Asian English

Natural patterns influenced by Bengali, Hindi, Tamil, and other local languages. Warm and formal simultaneously.

**Vocabulary:** "do the needful," "prepone," "kindly" (instead of "please"), "revert" (meaning "reply"), "out of station," "updation," "avail," "pass out" (meaning "graduate"), "lakh/crore," "timepass"

**Syntax patterns:**
- Present continuous with stative verbs: "I am having a doubt"
- Extra prepositions: "discuss about"
- "itself" as emphatic: "today itself"
- Tag questions: "You're coming, no?" / "na?"
- "only" for emphasis: "I told him only"

**Email patterns:** "PFA," "Kindly revert at the earliest," "Hoping for a positive response"

## Academic/Professional

Measured and hedged but still human. Not sterile.

**Hedging words:** "suggests," "appears to," "may indicate," "arguably," "it could be contended"

**Latin terms:** "et al.," "i.e.," "e.g.," "viz.," "inter alia"

**Patterns:**
- Deliberate passive voice: "It was observed that…" / "The data were analyzed…"
- Longer complex sentences with multiple subordinate clauses
- Citation-heavy
- Humor is dry and understated
- Opinions still present but carefully hedged

**Human academic writing is NOT the same as AI academic writing.** Real academics have strong opinions, use humor, reference specific papers by name, and engage in intellectual disputes. AI academic writing is generic hedging with no personality.

## AI/ML Twitter

Bridges deep technical knowledge with accessible energy. Paper-drop culture.

**What performs well:**
- Paper summaries with accessible TL;DRs
- Demo videos of new capabilities
- Benchmark comparisons and analysis
- Hot takes on model releases ("just dropped" energy)
- Tutorial threads on using new models
- Comparison threads (Model A vs B for task X)

**Writing style:** Mix of technical depth and casual energy. Paper citations explained plainly. Demo-first approach. Acknowledges complexity without being gatekeeping.

**Vocabulary:** "SOTA," "fine-tuning," "inference," "context window," "RAG," "LoRA," "hallucination," "alignment," "eval," "benchmark," "token budget," "prompt engineering," "system prompt"

**Example:** "New paper from DeepMind. They achieved SOTA on [benchmark] by [method]. The key insight: [one-liner]."

**What to avoid:** "this changes EVERYTHING" hype without substance. Either too academic (impenetrable) or too hype-driven. What's underserved: practical insights on using AI tools effectively day-to-day.

## Personality injection

When writing for a specific persona, calibrate these dimensions:

**Voice dimensions (scale 1–10):**
- Formality
- Humor
- Directness
- Jargon density
- Emotional expressiveness
- Sentence length preference
- Confidence level
- Warmth

**Assign verbal tics per persona:**
- Pet words/phrases: "literally," "honestly," "look," "fundamentally"
- Filler words: "like," "I mean," "you know," "right?"
- Greeting style: "Hey!" vs "Good morning" vs "gm" vs "Yo"
- Intensifiers: "super," "very," "incredibly," "lowkey," "mad" (NY)

**Define structural habits:**
- Short vs long paragraphs
- Bullet points vs flowing prose
- Questions vs statements
- Parenthetical aside frequency
- Capitalization for emphasis vs italics
